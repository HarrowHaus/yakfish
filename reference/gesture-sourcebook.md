# Wire: A Design Sourcebook for Dethroning Scroll and Completing an Absorption Topology

## TL;DR
- **The discrete, committed, per-item advance corner is already inhabited on touch** — by the TikTok/Reels/Shorts snap-swipe (one video per ballistic flick) and by e-reader tap zones (Kindle "EasyReach," Kobo) — proving the thumb-equivalent of vim's j/k can be dominant *without* degrading into a Tinder card-stack, because the advance axis carries pure sequencing (next/previous), not decision semantics. For Wire, the recommended dominant gesture is a target-free, ballistic, single-action committed advance with a snap boundary and haptic confirmation, with reversibility preserved via a non-blocking undo rather than a confirmation prompt.
- **The physics floor sets hard limits:** the dominant gesture must be target-free (no aiming — Fitts' Index of Difficulty driven toward zero), ballistic/open-loop, carry at most one coupled action (Hick's practical 2–3 primary-choice cap reserved for rare secondary actions), and provide immediate tactile/haptic terminal confirmation to canonize into a motor program (Schmidt schema theory). Reversibility is the genuinely open axis: irreversibility helps by forcing committed attention but harms through habituation, so Wire should make the gesture *feel* committed while keeping it *technically* reversible.
- **Channel budget per element is roughly 3–4 simultaneous pre-attentive visual channels plus ~1 robust haptic channel**, each carrying ~3 reliably distinguishable levels; position is the strongest visual channel, rhythm the strongest haptic one, and frequency is largely unusable on phone actuators. Authorship in Wire is located not in look but in the *absorption topology* — which functions fold onto which host elements — exactly as generative-music and parametric-design traditions locate signature in structural rather than aesthetic choices.

## Key Findings

1. **The highest-priority question resolves affirmatively.** A discrete, committed, per-item advance gesture *is* dominant on touch today. The TikTok-family vertical swipe is implemented as snap-to-item pagination (one full-screen video per flick), not continuous scroll; it is target-free, ballistic, committed, per-item, and globally dominant — and it never became a Tinder card-stack because its axis encodes sequence, not a binary decision. E-reader tap zones (Kindle's invisible "EasyReach" zones; Kobo's tap/swipe page turn) are the purest tap-based j/k analog and were chosen for paged reading specifically because tapping is low-precision, one-handed, and committed.

2. **Scroll's five properties are separable, and the under-inhabited corners are the opportunity.** Scroll is continuous, unbounded, low-effort, reversible, and decoupled-from-commitment. Wire's link-aggregator economics (it owns no content; commitment is to attention-allocation, not content-fate) make the *bounded / metered-effort / discrete / coupled-to-commitment* corners not just habitable but advantageous.

3. **The physics floor is strict and well-quantified.** Fitts' Law rewards target-free ballistic gestures; Hick's Law caps simultaneous choices; marking-menu research gives the hard motor-learning numbers (3.5× speed gain from mark over menu; under-10% error needs ≤8 items at depth 2 or ≤4 items at depth 4); Schmidt's schema theory explains why terminal haptic confirmation closes the motor-learning loop.

4. **3D Touch is the canonical failure mode to avoid:** undiscoverable, requiring grip-preparation and graded pressure, ergonomically fatiguing, and saving no steps — it violated every physics-floor constraint and was killed.

5. **The channel budget is measurable, not a guess:** ~3–4 pre-attentive visual channels and ~1 robust haptic channel, ~3 levels each.

## Details

### PART 1 — The Scroll-Property Generator

Scroll occupies the corner {continuous, unbounded, low-effort, reversible, decoupled-from-commitment}. Mapping the 2⁵ = 32 corners:

- **Continuous vs. discrete.** Continuous: scroll, the iPod click wheel, the Apple Watch Digital Crown, jog wheels, sliders. These gained fluid rate-control and lost addressability — you cannot land on item *n* without visual closed-loop correction. Discrete: pagination, e-reader page-turn, keyboard j/k, TikTok snap-swipe. These gained per-item addressability and a clean mental map and lost rate-control. The Frontiers (Mangen et al., 2019) Kindle study and the CHI 2025 smartphone study show discretization into pre-defined pages "makes it easier for readers to build a better mental representation of the text," [Nikhitajoshi](https://nikhitajoshi.ca/papers/scroll-vs-page.pdf) because scrolling "is known to impede readers' capacity to create an effective mental map of the text." [Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2019.00038/full)

- **Unbounded vs. bounded.** Unbounded scroll enables infinite feeds (and grazing). Bounded traversal (a deck of N items, a finite page count) gives a finishable task. For an aggregator whose value is *attention triage*, boundedness converts an endless graze into a completable session — the single most important inversion for Wire.

- **Low-effort vs. metered-effort.** Scroll's flick is near-zero-effort, which is why it dethroned page-down — but near-zero effort is precisely what enables mindless grazing. Metered effort (a click-wheel detent, a Digital Crown's haptic notch, a deliberate page-tap) imposes a small per-item cost that throttles consumption rate. The Apple Watch Series 4 Digital Crown added "continuous haptic feedback … tiny haptics and sounds guide you through," [9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2018/10/04/apple-watch-series-4-digital-crown-evolution-review/) making each increment *felt*.

- **Reversible vs. committed.** The open, high-stakes axis (see Part 6 resolution).

- **Decoupled vs. coupled-to-commitment.** Scroll is decoupled: moving past an item commits to nothing. Coupling advance to a lightweight commitment ("I have seen and dismissed this") is the triage grammar of Superhuman/Hey — but on touch it has only been realized as Tinder-style binary decision, which Wire must avoid.

**Inhabited corners:** the scroll corner; the discrete-bounded-metered-committed corner (e-readers, paged manga, Stories, TikTok). **Empty-but-possible corner:** discrete + bounded + metered + *reversible* + coupled — a finishable, per-item, effort-metered traversal where each advance lightly commits but is undoable. This is the candidate gesture for Wire.

### PART 2 — The Physics Floor

**Fitts' Law.** Movement time rises with the log of distance-over-target-width (Index of Difficulty). A *target-free* gesture (swipe/flick anywhere) drives ID toward zero — the reason scroll, and the TikTok swipe, are so fast. On touch, the system cannot know where the thumb is, so "no part of the screen is closer to the pointer than another" [Smashing Magazine](https://www.smashingmagazine.com/2022/02/fitts-law-touch-era/) (Smashing Magazine, *Fitts' Law In The Touch Era*) — aimed targets are expensive, target-free gestures cheap. **Hard constraint: the dominant gesture must be target-free.**

**Ballistic vs. closed-loop.** Fitts movements are a ballistic launch plus closed-loop correction (IxDF). A canonizable dominant gesture must complete in the *ballistic* phase — no mid-course visual correction. The TikTok flick and the keyboard keypress are ballistic; landing on a small button is not.

**Hick's Law and the choice cap.** Decision time rises logarithmically with the number of equiprobable choices (Hick 1952; Hyman 1953). Practical UX guidance is to limit each decision step to two or three primary choices. **Hard constraint: the dominant high-frequency gesture should carry exactly ONE action (advance); secondary actions should be capped at 2–4 and reserved for low-frequency moments.** This validates framing assumption (b): physics should veto a high-frequency gesture that demands a choice, but only flag low-frequency secondary ones.

**Power law of practice + Schmidt schema theory.** Motor performance improves as a power function of practice; a generalized motor program (GMP) stores invariant relative timing/force and is parameterized per execution (Schmidt 1975). Crucially, "an error signal is sent upon finalizing the movement, where the schema is then modified based on the sensory feedback and knowledge of results." [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motor_program) **Hard constraint: the gesture needs immediate, consistent terminal feedback (haptic + snap) so the schema forms — this is precisely what scroll's rubber-band bounce and the Digital Crown's detent provide, and what 3D Touch failed to provide.**

### PART 3 — Gesture Lineages Surveyed Against the Floor

**(a) How dominant gestures canonized.** Pointing/clicking (Macintosh mouse), tapping (iPhone multitouch), scrolling (inertial flick) each stuck because they were target-free or large-target, ballistic, single-action, and immediately feedback-confirmed. Apple itself frames the lineage: "the iPod introduced the click wheel … multitouch on the iPhone made using a touchscreen feel natural. The Digital Crown was positioned as the next transformative input device." [9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2018/10/04/apple-watch-series-4-digital-crown-evolution-review/)

**(b) Bounded continuous controls.** The click wheel "worked because it was based on fundamental principles of human interaction … Humans are naturally better at continuous motions than repetitive clicking." [Bytole](https://www.bytole.com/insights/design/why-apples-ipod-click-wheel-is-a-design-masterpiece/) The Digital Crown offloaded scroll/zoom off the small screen so "fingers [didn't obscure] the display." [AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/15/05/25/watch-effect-how-the-digital-crown-and-force-touch-could-inform-future-ios-device-design) **The unanswered touchscreen-dial question:** no one has shipped a great touchscreen rotary control because on-screen knobs lack the mechanical detent and fixed pivot — users cannot tell rotation direction or value without looking ("I have no idea … except if I try and see," [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=41961096) Hacker News), whereas "a linear slider … is impossible to misunderstand." [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=41961096) Hardware hybrids (Microchip's Knob-on-Display, XeelTech HAPTICORE, Panasonic Magic Knob) re-add a physical passive knob over the glass precisely because the touchscreen alone cannot supply the proprioceptive/haptic closed loop. **Lesson for Wire: a rotary metaphor on glass is a dead end; the metered-effort feeling must come from haptics + snap, not a simulated dial.**

**(c) Pre-scroll navigation.** Page-down, pagination, hyperlink-to-next, framesets — all discrete and bounded, all lost to scroll because they were higher-effort and target-dependent (you had to aim at a "next" control). Their virtue (clean addressability, mental map) is exactly what Wire wants to recover *without* their target-dependence — i.e., make the discrete advance target-free.

**(d) Touchscreen gestures beyond scroll.** Pinch-zoom and edge-swipe-back stuck (target-free, ballistic, single meaning); iOS 26 even loosened the back-swipe to start "from the middle of the screen," [Medium](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/who-decided-where-the-back-button-goes-and-why-didnt-they-ask-anyone-who-actually-holds-a-phone-d7e81ac636c7) and Android's back works "from both edges … that's Fitts' Law in practice." [Medium](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/who-decided-where-the-back-button-goes-and-why-didnt-they-ask-anyone-who-actually-holds-a-phone-d7e81ac636c7) Long-press is slow (time-gated, not ballistic). Rotate and multi-finger swipes are undiscoverable and grip-dependent. **3D Touch/Force Touch was killed** because it was "exceptionally undiscoverable," required users to "prepare your grip in advance … and hold it tight while trying to control the level of press," [The Mobile Spoon](https://www.mobilespoon.net/2016/10/heres-why-you-dont-use-iphones-3d-touch.html) was "an ergonomic nightmare," [The Mobile Spoon](https://www.mobilespoon.net/2016/10/heres-why-you-dont-use-iphones-3d-touch.html) saved no steps (force-touch icon → tap shortcut → compose was still three steps), and added display cost/complexity. Apple replaced it with Haptic Touch (a long-press), which is "slower" and "you're never going to find it by chance." [AppleInsider](https://appleinsider.com/articles/19/10/02/editorial-apples-removal-of-3d-touch-is-a-backwards-step-for-pro-iphones) **This is the master failure case: a gesture that is graded (not ballistic), grip-dependent (not target-free), undiscoverable, and feedback-ambiguous cannot canonize.**

**(e) Per-item-commitment grammars — the highest-priority sub-question.** On the *keyboard*, triage tools have perfected committed per-item advance: Superhuman ships "100+ keyboard shortcuts … Press c to compose, e to archive, j and k to navigate," [Superhuman](https://blog.superhuman.com/gmail-vs-superhuman/) teaches them via a Cmd-K palette that "makes itself obsolete for power users," [Blake Crosley](https://blakecrosley.com/guides/design/superhuman) drills them in onboarding ("don't explain features—they drill muscle memory"), [Blake Crosley](https://blakecrosley.com/guides/design/superhuman) and uses optimistic UI so "actions complete visually before the server responds, with undo as the safety net." [Blake Crosley](https://blakecrosley.com/guides/design/superhuman) The j/k vim model gives a spatial home-row mental model. **On touch, the question is whether discrete committed per-item advance became dominant without becoming a card-swipe — and the answer is yes:**
  - **TikTok/Reels/Shorts vertical swipe** is snap-to-item (one video per ballistic flick; "each vertical swipe acts as a natural punctuation mark between content pieces"), [Passionate Agency](https://passionates.com/tiktok-revolution-smart-design-built-250b-empire/) implemented as virtualized snap-pagination (Mux engineering), not free scroll. It removed left/right decision semantics, "streamlin[ing] the user's decision process to a simple … watch or scroll." [Passionate Agency](https://passionates.com/tiktok-revolution-smart-design-built-250b-empire/) Target-free, ballistic, committed, per-item, dominant — and *not* a Tinder stack, because the axis is pure sequence.
  - **E-reader tap zones** (Kindle's invisible "EasyReach" zones: tap right two-thirds = next page, narrow left = previous; Kobo's tap/swipe with an explicit "prevent accidental page turns" setting) are the purest tap-based j/k. Chosen because tapping "anywhere … reduces the fine motor precision traditionally required," [Insiderbits](https://insiderbits.com/tips/kindle/) aiding one-handed and accessibility use.
  - **Stories tap-to-advance** (Instagram right-tap = forward). Snapchat replaced its swipe-up affordance with tap-through in 2021; the rationale "People tap more than they swipe … Tapping feels faster on mobile" [Choto Blog](https://blog.choto.co/snapchat-swipe-up-vs-tap-through/) traces to a promotional blog (Choto.co) rather than Snap primary documentation, so treat the *motivation* as secondary even though the switch itself is real.
  - **The documented limit:** Instagram *deliberately tested* tap-to-advance in the **Explore tab** with a small number of users in October 2018 (TechCrunch / Josh Constine); the appearance in the *main feed* on December 27, 2018 was an over-broad accidental rollout of that test — Instagram: "Due to a bug, some users saw a change to the way their feed appears today. We quickly fixed the issue." [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2018/12/27/instagram-tap-through-posts-bug/) Discrete advance wins for bounded, full-screen, one-item-at-a-time content but scroll retains the open-ended mixed feed. **For Wire — a bounded set of discrete links — this is precisely the winning condition.**

**(f) Game UI gesture invention.** Marking menus (Gordon Kurtenbach & Bill Buxton) are the transferable jewel: a novice pops up a radial menu; with practice the same motion becomes a blind ballistic "mark." Per Kurtenbach & Buxton, *User Learning and Performance with Marking Menus* (Proc. CHI '94, pp. 258–264): "Using a mark on average was 3.5 times faster than selection using the menu … marking was relatively error free." [Big Medium](https://bigmedium.com/ideas/radial-menus-for-touch-ui.html) Limits: the same paper warns "marking menus are not appropriate when the list of items changes dynamically," [Big Medium](https://bigmedium.com/ideas/radial-menus-for-touch-ui.html) and (per Zhao & Balakrishnan, *Simple vs. Compound Mark Hierarchical Marking Menus*, UIST 2004) for under-10% error you can have ≤4 items at 4 levels deep or ≤8 items at 2 levels. [University of Toronto](https://www.dgp.toronto.edu/~ravin/papers/uist2004_simplemm.pdf) The rehearsal principle (novice and expert motions are identical) is the transfer condition: Wire's onboarding gesture must *be* the expert gesture, just slower.

**(g) E-reader page-turn tradition.** Pagination was chosen over scroll for long reading because discretization builds a better mental map and supplies kinesthetic/positional cues; the Frontiers (2019) study stripped visual length cues and found "sensorimotor cues play a role when reading a print book, whereas they are lacking when reading an e-book." [Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2019.00038/full) **Reveals: scroll fits open-ended skim/graze; discrete advance fits bounded, comprehension-oriented, one-unit-at-a-time consumption — which is exactly the link-triage task.**

**(h) Voice and command-palette.** Cmd-K palettes (Raycast, Linear, GitHub, Notion) canonize "express intent, content materializes" when the palette shows the shortcut every time, teaching graduation to direct invocation; Raycast aliases use "strict prefix matching … ideal for muscle-memory workflows." [Raycast](https://manual.raycast.com/command-aliases-and-hotkeys) For Wire, a palette is the right *secondary* (low-frequency) channel — not the dominant gesture.

**(i) Spatial/gaze/accelerometer.** Gaze+swipe hybrids (GazeSwipe, CHI 2025) and reachability research show large screens leave only ~40% thumb-reachable on phones ≥6.5″, [Medium](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/who-decided-where-the-back-button-goes-and-why-didnt-they-ask-anyone-who-actually-holds-a-phone-d7e81ac636c7) motivating target-free gestures over aimed targets. Transfer to 2D: rely on gross direction, not precise endpoints.

**(j) Haptics as gesture-completer and information channel.** Haptics close the Schmidt feedback loop (terminal confirmation) and can carry information. Rhythm is the most robust tacton parameter (Hoggan, Anwar & Brewster report a 2-note rhythm averaging 98.33% recognition and "no significant differences in error rates between three different rhythms"); [Joemon Jose](https://www.dcs.gla.ac.uk/~stephen/papers/HAID2.pdf) roughness/intensity reach ~80% at 3 levels. Per Hoggan & Brewster, *New Parameters for Tacton Design* (Proc. CHI 2007), recognition for **waveform was 94.2%, for frequency only 81%, and for amplitude modulation only 61.1%** — so encode roughness via waveform, not raw amplitude, and treat **frequency as largely unusable on phone actuators** (skin range 10–400 Hz; phone motors resonant ~250 Hz). Per Brown, Brewster & Purchase, *Multidimensional Tactons* (MobileHCI '06), two-parameter tactons (rhythm + roughness) "can be understood by users, with recognition rates of around 70%"; [columbia](http://www.cs.columbia.edu/~coms6998-11/papers/Brown_MobHCI06.pdf) three-parameter tactons fall to ~48% (recoverable to ~81% by trimming one parameter's levels). **Budget: ~1 robust haptic channel, ~3 levels, kept short (~300 ms).**

**(k) Anti-engagement / digital-wellness.** Forest, Opal, Screen Time add friction to grazing. The mechanism Wire should borrow is *boundedness + metered effort*: a finishable session and a small per-item cost are intrinsic anti-grazing levers, structurally rather than via blocking overlays.

### PART 4 — Principle Stream (functional/structural only)

- **Function-invariance / morphological method** (Goethe, D'Arcy Thompson, Propp, Marr's three levels, Alexander's pattern language, Jobs-to-be-Done): separate the *function* (traverse-and-triage) from its current *form* (scroll). Marr's levels — computational (what/why), algorithmic (how represented), implementational (physical) — let Wire fix the computational goal (allocate attention across a bounded set) and re-choose the algorithm (discrete committed advance) and implementation (thumb flick + haptic). Got right: invariants survive surface change. Failure mode: morphological reasoning can over-abstract and ignore device-specific motor constraints.
- **Earned chrome / minimum-presence** (Calm Technology — Weiser, Brown, Case; progressive disclosure — Nielsen, Tognazzini; Norman affordances/signifiers; Tufte data-ink ratio as principle). Calm tech principles: technology "should require the smallest possible amount of attention," "should make use of the periphery," [Adobe](https://blog.adobe.com/en/publish/2016/10/12/calm-technology-in-the-era-of-experience-design) and "the right amount of technology is the minimum needed to solve the problem." [Adobe](https://blog.adobe.com/en/publish/2016/10/12/calm-technology-in-the-era-of-experience-design) Tufte: maximize data-ink, "erase non-data-ink … within reason." [KPIInstitute](https://www.performancemagazine.org/data-ink-ratio-minimalism-data-visualization/) Failure mode: stripping signifiers (flat design, 3D Touch) removes discoverability — affordance must survive the erase.
- **Lawful systems as substrate** (Utopia fluid type/space scales, Vignelli grid, Müller-Brockmann Swiss grid, modular scales): a rule system guarantees coherence so the designer's choices become structural. Failure: rigid grids fight genuinely variable content.
- **Multi-channel encoding** (variable fonts' axis ecology — weight/width/optical-size/grade; Bertin's retinal variables; Cleveland-McGill ranking; Tufte small multiples; instrument panels). Cleveland-McGill accuracy ranking: position > length > angle/slope > area > volume > color/density. The grade axis changes weight "without changing the overall space the text occupies" [ACM Digital Library](https://dl.acm.org/doi/fullHtml/10.1145/3544548.3581552) (no reflow) — a free emphasis channel. Failure: overloading one element past the channel budget.
- **Non-editorial honest surfaces** (library card catalogs, indie-web, terminal readers, librarianship neutrality): present items without editorial weighting; the surface's job is faithful indexing, not persuasion. Fits Wire's no-agenda constraint.
- **Honest fast-egress economics** (early Google homepage, directories, search-result surfaces designed for departure, slow-web): the surface succeeds when the user *leaves quickly* for the right link. Wire owns no content, so its success metric is fast correct egress, not dwell.
- **Reduce-then-rebuild from emptiness** (Bauhaus Vorkurs of Itten/Albers, Shaker, Enzo Mari's Autoprogettazione, Japanese *ma*, Donald Judd): start from nothing, add only what earns its place; negative space (*ma*) is load-bearing. Failure: minimalism as style rather than as function-driven subtraction.
- **Concentration that unfolds** (Vignelli NYC subway map, sparklines, dense info graphics): high information density that stays legible because structure (not decoration) carries the load.

### PART 5 — The Intersection Matrix (principle × surviving motor mechanism)

Surviving motor mechanisms (pass the physics floor): **(M1)** target-free ballistic directional flick with snap (TikTok-type); **(M2)** target-free tap-zone advance (e-reader EasyReach); **(M3)** marking-menu radial mark for low-frequency secondary actions; **(M4)** haptic terminal confirmation; **(M5)** command palette for rare intent.

Populated cells (habitable example exists):
- Calm tech × M2 (tap-advance) → Kindle EasyReach: right (peripheral, minimal attention); wrong (undocumented — discoverability suffers).
- Data-ink × M1 → TikTok full-screen one-item: right (whole screen is "data," no chrome); wrong (autoplay couples to engagement, not user intent).
- Multi-channel encoding × M4 (haptic) → Digital Crown detents: right (felt metering); wrong (only on dedicated hardware).
- Marking menus × M3 → games/Maya: right (rehearsal → ballistic); wrong (breaks if item set is dynamic).
- Lawful systems × M1 → snap-pagination grid: right (bounded addressable items).

**Empty cells flagged as candidate gestures (unbuilt-but-possible):**
- **Reduce-from-emptiness × M1+M4+bounded set:** a *finishable*, target-free ballistic per-item advance over a bounded link deck, each advance haptic-confirmed and lightly committed (item marked "seen") but undoable. *Not impossible — merely unbuilt for an aggregator.* What it takes: snap-pagination over a bounded set + optimistic "seen" state + non-blocking undo + a single terminal haptic. **This is Wire's dominant-gesture candidate.**
- **Marking menu × triage on touch:** a radial mark for the 2–4 secondary actions (save / open / hide-source) invoked from the same advancing thumb, replacing the Tinder left/right binary. Possible; unbuilt at scale.

Cells that are genuinely impossible: touchscreen rotary dial as dominant input (no proprioceptive closed loop — stated reason in Part 3b); graded-pressure dominant gesture (3D Touch — not ballistic, not discoverable).

### PART 6 — The Recursive Earning Engine (function-folding vocabulary)

**(1) Beauty that survives removal of appearance-decisions** — structural beauty: the Shaker chair, suspension bridge, Eames lounge, Vignelli subway map, Olivetti typewriter, soap film. Each is beautiful *because* form is the precipitate of functional honesty (the soap film minimizes area; the bridge cable traces the funicular of load). Wire's analog: the gesture's "beauty" must be the precipitate of the physics floor — target-free because aiming is waste, ballistic because correction is waste, single-action because choice is waste.

**(2) Beauty that evaporates when appearance is removed** — test whether the underlying function is real. A simulated leather texture: function not real → discard (pastiche). A simulated rotary knob on glass: function (rate control) real, but the *form* fails on touch → keep function, change form (fold rate-metering into haptics).

**(3) Function-folding / edge-collapse vocabulary** (a separate component's job folded into a host until the standalone component disappears):
- **Scrollbar → absorbed into the advance gesture's snap + a peripheral progress glyph** (position channel, Cleveland-McGill's strongest variable).
- **"Next" button → absorbed into the target-free flick/tap** (the control disappears; the whole surface becomes the control — as TikTok and EasyReach do).
- **"Mark as seen" affordance → absorbed into the advance itself** (advancing past = seen; optimistic UI, undo as safety net — the Superhuman move).
- **Confirmation dialog → absorbed into a non-blocking undo toast** (Raskin/Cooper/ESR Rule of Reversibility).
- **Emphasis/weight markers → absorbed into the variable-font grade axis** (weight change without reflow).
- **Separate haptic "ping" → absorbed into the gesture's terminal detent** (one event carries both completion and metering).

**Cataloged failure cases where appearance was load-bearing (pastiche to refuse):** skeuomorphic chrome/wood/leather (pixels-were-expensive rationale, now obsolete); the on-screen rotary knob; graded-pressure 3D Touch; any "engagement" animation whose only job is to reward grazing.

### PART 7 — Channel-Budget Measurement

Defensible per-element budget, broken down:
- **Visual, pre-attentive (processed in <200–250 ms, "felt not noticed"):** ~3–4 simultaneous channels before they stop being pre-attentive and require serial attention. Ware's pop-out channels are roughly orthogonal in three families (form: position/size/orientation/length; color: hue/luminance; motion). Cleveland-McGill accuracy order: **position > length > angle/slope > area > volume > color-saturation/density**. Practical budget: **position (1, strongest) + one form channel (size or weight) + one color channel (hue or luminance) + optionally motion = 3–4 channels**, each with ~3 reliably separable steps for ordinal use.
- **Typographic sub-channels (variable fonts):** weight, optical size, and **grade** (weight-without-reflow) are independent axes; grade is the cheapest emphasis channel because it changes typographic color without layout shift. Treat weight+grade as ~1–1.5 usable emphasis channels at ~2–3 JND-separated steps.
- **Haptic:** **~1 robust channel** (rhythm, ≈90%+ at 3 levels — a 2-note rhythm averaged 98.33% in Hoggan/Anwar/Brewster), expandable to a degraded second (roughness/intensity ~80% at 3 levels; encode via waveform — 94.2% — not amplitude — 61.1%); **frequency unusable** on phone actuators; two-parameter tactons ≈70%, keep ≤300 ms.
- **Spatial/position:** the single strongest channel across both Bertin and Cleveland-McGill — Wire should spend its richest encoding on *position*.

**Headline number: budget ≈ 3–4 visual + 1 haptic simultaneous channels per element, ~3 levels each.** This is the measured ceiling the absorption step must respect — folding more than ~4 jobs onto one element pushes it past pre-attentive into serial-search territory.

### PART 8 — Absorption Topology as Authorship

The earning procedure is confluent only up to absorption *order*: the same functions folded in different orders yield different but equally-valid fixed-points. Therefore Wire's identity = *which functions fold onto which host elements*. Prior art for locating authorship structurally rather than aesthetically:
- **Generative/algorithmic music** (Koenig, Ames, Eno's "generative music," constraint+Markov systems): "rules guarantee coherence and the composer's choices are structural"; "in order to follow the rules, one will often devise creative solutions that might not otherwise have been chosen … we are still left with the question of authorship" [University of Maryland](https://user.eng.umd.edu/~blj/algorithmic_composition/algorithmicmodel.html) (Univ. of Maryland, *Algorithmic Composition as a Model of Creativity*). The composer authors the *ruleset and the folding*, not each note.
- **Parametric/generative architecture and constraint-based layout** (and Müller-Brockmann/Vignelli grids): signature lives in the constraint system and the priority order of folds.
- **Constraint-based composition** (NeuralConstraints, Frontiers 2025): a solver guarantees validity; the author chooses *which* constraints dominate.

**Discipline for choosing a good absorption topology:** (i) fold higher-frequency functions onto the dominant gesture first (Hick/Fitts priority); (ii) never fold past the channel budget (Part 7 ceiling); (iii) fold a function only if its standalone form fails the structural-beauty test (Part 6); (iv) preserve reversibility when folding commitment (Part 6 / Part 2). The *order* of these folds is Wire's authored signature.

### Resolution of the open reversible-vs-committed question

The evidence converges on a clear position: **make the gesture phenomenologically committed but technically reversible.** Three strands:
1. **Committed feels right for triage and attention.** Irreversible interactions force "prospective thinking and mindful acting" [ACM Digital Library](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3544548.3581433) (CHI 2023, *Point of No Undo*); friction can improve decision quality under repeated commitments (arXiv 2026, blockchain confirmation study). A link-aggregator's commitment is to *attention-allocation, not content-fate* — so the cost of a "wrong" advance is near-zero, which means full irreversibility buys little and risks much.
2. **True irreversibility harms through habituation.** Aza Raskin, Alan Cooper, and Eric Raymond converge: "Every confirmation prompt should be a surprise"; "Every operation without an undo is a horror story waiting to happen"; [Catb](http://www.catb.org/~esr/writings/taouu/html/ch04s04.html) confirmations "only work when they are unexpected," [Designsmarts](https://designsmarts.co/confirm-or-undo) and routine ones get dismissed reflexively — turning habituation "into a weapon against the user's interests." [Catb](http://www.catb.org/~esr/writings/taouu/html/ch04s04.html)
3. **The synthesis is the Superhuman optimistic-UI pattern:** the advance commits *visually and immediately* (snap + haptic + "seen" state), giving the felt finality that throttles grazing and builds the motor schema, while a *non-blocking undo* (not a prompt) preserves recoverability. This inhabits the empty corner from Part 1: discrete + bounded + metered + coupled-to-commitment + *reversible*.

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Ship the dominant gesture (now).** Make Wire's primary content-traversal a **target-free, ballistic, snap-paginated per-item advance** over a *bounded* deck of links (one link-card per screen or per discrete advance), with a single terminal **haptic detent** confirming each advance. Model it on the TikTok snap-swipe + Kindle EasyReach tap-zone, *not* on free scroll. Benchmark to change course: if a completion event fires for fewer than ~30% of sessions, the deck is not bounded enough.

**Stage 2 — Make commitment felt but reversible.** Advancing past a link marks it "seen" optimistically; provide a **non-blocking undo** (toast, or swipe-back), never a confirmation prompt (habituation makes prompts useless — Raskin/Cooper/ESR). Benchmark: if accidental-advance complaints exceed ~2% of advances, add a snap threshold or light metered effort; if users report "too much friction," reduce the detent.

**Stage 3 — Absorb secondary actions into a radial mark, capped at 2–4.** Replace any Tinder-style left/right binary with a **marking-menu-style radial** for save / open-externally / hide-source, invoked from the same thumb. Keep ≤4 items so it stays under the 10%-error, single-level limit (Zhao & Balakrishnan) and so the novice motion equals the expert mark (rehearsal). Benchmark: if expert users still pop the menu (don't "go blind") after ~2 weeks, the item count or invocation delay is wrong.

**Stage 4 — Spend the channel budget deliberately.** Encode the most important variable in **position** (strongest channel), use **variable-font grade** for reflow-free emphasis, reserve **one hue/luminance** channel and **one haptic rhythm** channel. Do not exceed ~3–4 visual + 1 haptic channels per card.

**Stage 5 — Author the absorption topology.** Document, in order, which functions fold onto the advance gesture (next-button, mark-seen, progress, haptic confirmation) and which remain standalone. This ordered fold-list *is* Wire's signature — treat it like a generative composer's ruleset.

**What would change these recommendations:** If A/B data shows discrete advance materially lowers link-throughput vs. scroll for Wire's specific bounded deck sizes, fall back to a *hybrid* (discrete advance within a section, scroll between sections) — mirroring manga readers' paged-vs-webtoon split. If haptic actuators on target devices prove too weak for a felt detent, shift metering to a visual snap + audio tick.

## Caveats

- **Several gesture-rationale sources are secondary or promotional** (agency blogs on TikTok "embodied cognition/dopamine," Medium essays on 3D Touch, and the Snapchat swipe→tap *motivation* which traces to Choto.co rather than Snap docs). The *factual* claims (TikTok one-video snap; Snapchat's 2021 switch; Instagram's 2018 Explore tap-test and the December bug-driven feed appearance; 3D Touch removal) are corroborated by reputable outlets (TechCrunch, AppleInsider, PhoneArena) and primary docs; the interpretive framing is not measured.
- **Haptic discrimination numbers** come from Brewster/Brown/Hoggan studies often placing actuators on forearm/back, not a thumb gripping a phone; phone-in-hand fingertip discrimination may differ, and absolute (no-comparison) identification is materially harder than relative discrimination.
- **Reading-comprehension pagination-vs-scroll findings are mixed**: the CHI 2025 smartphone study found no significant comprehension difference, while older desktop studies and the Frontiers (2019) Kindle study favor pagination's mental-map benefit. The robust claim is about *mental map / addressability*, not raw comprehension.
- **Marking-menu numbers** (3.5× speed; ≤8@2 / ≤4@4 for <10% error) are from peer-reviewed primary sources (Kurtenbach & Buxton, CHI '94; Zhao & Balakrishnan, UIST 2004) and are solid.
- **The channel-budget headline (~3–4 visual + 1 haptic)** is a defensible synthesis of Ware/Bertin/Cleveland-McGill/Brewster, not a single measured constant; exact counts depend on element size, viewing time, and inter-channel orthogonality.
- Planned searches on radial-menu touch deployments at consumer scale and on digital-wellness gesture internals beyond the mechanism level were not completed within budget; those points are reasoned from adjacent sources and flagged accordingly.
